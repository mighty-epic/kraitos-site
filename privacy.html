Privacy Policy

LAST UPDATED: JUNE 12, 2026

Kraitos ("we," "our," or "us") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you visit our website at kraitos.app, join our waitlist, or use the Kraitos desktop AI operator ("Software").

1. Information We Collect

We may collect information about you in a variety of ways. The information we may collect includes:

  • Personal Data: Personally identifiable information, such as your email address, which you voluntarily provide when joining the Kraitos waitlist.
  • Device and Telemetry Data: Information about your computer hardware and software that may be collected during download or beta operation. This may include your IP address, browser type, operating system type, installation status, and diagnostics.
  • Cookies and Local Storage: We use functional local storage keys (such as waitlist completion flags and rate-limiting timestamp logs) to manage your session and prevent spam. We do not use tracking or advertising cookies.

2. Use of Your Information

Having accurate information about you allows us to provide you with a smooth, efficient, and customized experience. Specifically, we may use information collected about you to:

  • Send you beta verification links and confirmation emails.
  • Deliver and manage access to Software installers and updates.
  • Monitor and analyze usage and trends to improve user experience.
  • Prevent fraudulent transactions, monitor against spam, and protect against security threats.

3. Sharing and Disclosing Your Information

We do not sell, trade, or rent your personal data to others. We share data only with third-party processors necessary to operate our services:

  • Netlify: Hosts our website, serves code assets, and stores verified waitlist form submissions securely.
  • Resend: Used to dispatch transactional double opt-in emails and verification links.
  • Cloudflare: Provides CDN delivery, DDoS mitigation, and Turnstile bot challenge validations to protect our site from spam.

4. Security of Your Information

We use administrative, technical, and physical security measures to help protect your personal information. These include HTTP Strict Transport Security (HSTS) encryption, Content Security Policies (CSP), cryptographic token validation, and rate-limiting filters. While we have taken reasonable steps to secure the personal information you provide, please be aware that no security measures are perfect or impenetrable.

5. Your Rights (GDPR / CCPA Compliance)

Depending on your location, you may have rights regarding your personal data. These include the right to access, correct, or delete the personal data we hold about you. To exercise these rights, please contact us at the email address listed below.
